# 📊 Global Store Sales Analysis – Data Storytelling & Statistical Validation

---

## 🚀 Project Overview

This project analyzes a large-scale Global Store Sales dataset (~51K records) to uncover actionable insights related to sales performance, profitability drivers, customer behavior, and discount strategies.

The objective is to transform raw transactional data into a clear business narrative backed by statistical validation, enabling data-driven decision-making.

This project demonstrates an end-to-end data analytics workflow, including:

- Data cleaning & preprocessing  
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)  
- Data visualization  
- Hypothesis testing  
- Business storytelling  

---

## 🎯 Business Objective

Key business questions addressed:

- Which regions generate the highest sales and profit?  
- Which product categories drive maximum profitability?  
- What is the impact of discounts on profit margins?  
- Which customer segments contribute the most revenue?  

These insights help businesses optimize pricing, improve profitability, and target high-value customers effectively.

## 🔬 Hypothesis Testing

A Two-Sample T-Test was conducted to validate the impact of discounts on profitability.

### Hypothesis:

H₀: Discounts do not significantly affect profit  
H₁: High discounts significantly reduce profit  

### Result:

- p-value < 0.05  
- Null hypothesis rejected  

Conclusion: Higher discounts significantly reduce profitability  

Detailed analysis: hypothesis_testing_summary.md

## 📈 Key Business Insights

- High discounts negatively impact profit margins  
- Technology category generates the highest profit  
- Some regions show high sales but low profitability  
- Corporate customers contribute higher revenue per order  

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Limit excessive discounting (>20%)  
- Focus on high-margin categories (Technology)  
- Investigate low-profit regions  
- Target corporate customers with focused strategies
